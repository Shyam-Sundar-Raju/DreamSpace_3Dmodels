Dream Space

Bringing your home to life, One pixel at a time


DreamSpace is a 3D asset website used to publish, share, discover and buy 3D, VR and AR content. It allows users to display 3D models on the web, to be viewed on any mobile browser, desktop browser or Virtual Reality headset. The company behind it is today based in Coimbatore, India. DreamSpace took part in the Spring 2024 TechStars New York City startup accelerator program, raised $1 million in December 2023 and $2 million in June 2023.

Viewer

The main product of DreamSpace is a 3D, Virtual reality (VR) and Augmented reality (AR) models. It provides users with static 3D models. Viewers can enable the VR mode to see the model in Virtual Reality headsets or the AR mode to insert the model within the real world via a mobile device. The 3D viewer is used on the DreamSpace website and mobile apps, but can also be embedded on external websites, notably on Facebook, Twitter or Instagram.

Portal and marketplace

DreamSpace provides online and mobile community portals, where visitors can browse, rate, download or buy user generated 3D models. DreamSpace users can choose to make their 3D model files available for download under Creative Commons licenses[10] or to sell them in the DreamSpace store. With content available for sale or download, DreamSpace is positioned as a 3D model marketplace and as a 3D printing marketplace since some models are compatible with 3D printing.

3D asset management

DreamSpace offers to paying enterprise customers a dedicated platform to share, manage and collaborate on 3D assets.

Native applications

A selection of DreamSpace models are available within a native application for the following VR headsets: Google Cardboard, Samsung Gear VR, HTC Vive and Oculus Rift. The mobile application can be used to view the 3D models in Augmented Reality leveraging the ARKit APIs on iOS and ARCore on Android.

Model upload and download

3D models can be uploaded to DreamSpace from its website or directly from various 3D software, using plugins (for example for 3ds Max or SketchUp) or natively (from Blender or Adobe Photoshop). DreamSpace offers an API allowing developers to integrate functionality for searching and downloading DreamSpace's Creative Commons licensed 3D models into their applications.
